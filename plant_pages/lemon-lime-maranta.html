Lemon-Lime Maranta Care Guide

Maranta leuconeura 'Lemon Lime' - Prayer Plant

About Lemon-Lime Maranta

The Lemon-Lime Maranta, or Maranta leuconeura 'Lemon Lime', is a striking tropical plant known for its vibrant chartreuse leaves with dark green patterns. It's a member of the prayer plant family, named for the way its leaves fold upward at night, resembling hands in prayer.

âœ¨ Fun fact: Marantas move their leaves throughout the day in response to light, a process called nyctinasty. This movement is actually audible if you listen closely in a quiet room!

Light Requirements

Lemon-Lime Marantas thrive in medium to bright indirect light. Their vibrant coloration is most pronounced with adequate light, but direct sunlight can scorch their delicate leaves.

  • Ideal: Bright, indirect light
  • Avoid: Direct sunlight, especially during afternoon hours
  • Minimum: Can tolerate lower light, but may lose some variegation

Watering Needs

Keep the soil consistently moist but not soggy. Allow the top inch of soil to dry out slightly between waterings.

  • Spring/Summer: Water thoroughly when the top inch of soil feels dry
  • Fall/Winter: Reduce watering frequency as growth slows
  • Warning sign: Crispy leaf edges indicate underwatering; yellowing leaves may indicate overwatering

ðŸ’§ Tip: Use filtered water or leave tap water out overnight to allow chlorine to dissipate. Marantas can be sensitive to fluoride and chlorine in tap water.

Humidity & Temperature

As tropical plants, Lemon-Lime Marantas love humidity and warm temperatures.

  • Humidity: 50-60% or higher (use a humidifier or pebble tray)
  • Temperature: 65-80Â°F (18-27Â°C)
  • Avoid: Cold drafts, heating vents, and temperatures below 60Â°F (15Â°C)

Soil & Fertilizer

Lemon-Lime Marantas prefer well-draining, slightly acidic soil that retains some moisture.

  • Soil mix: 2 parts peat or coco coir, 1 part perlite, 1 part orchid bark
  • Fertilizer: Balanced houseplant fertilizer diluted to half-strength
  • Schedule: Feed monthly during growing season (spring and summer)

Common Problems

  • Curling leaves: Often indicates underwatering or low humidity
  • Brown leaf tips: Usually caused by low humidity or mineral buildup from tap water
  • Fading color: May indicate too much light or nutrient deficiency
  • Pests: Spider mites, mealybugs, and scale insects (inspect regularly)

Propagation

Lemon-Lime Marantas can be propagated through division or stem cuttings.

  1. Divide the plant during repotting, ensuring each section has roots and several leaves
  2. For stem cuttings, select a healthy stem with at least two leaves
  3. Place cuttings in water or moist soil and keep in warm, humid conditions
  4. Roots typically develop within 2-4 weeks

Enjoy your beautiful Lemon-Lime Maranta! With proper care, these plants can live for many years, bringing tropical beauty to your home.
